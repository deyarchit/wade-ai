Empowering individuals and teams to make mindful decisions with accurate, unbiased insights.

Motivation

In today's technological landscape, we are surrounded by numerous software tools designed to streamline various aspects of our work. These tools empower individuals and companies, simplifying processes from product ideation and design to development, marketing, and sales. However, amidst this abundance of tools, a fundamental challenge persists.

The Challenge

Thoughtful decision-making is crucial for success, and making effective future decisions requires accurate knowledge of past decision rationales, their outcomes, and the original context. Yet, the rationales and crucial context behind these decisions are often fragmented and difficult to access. We either attempt to piece this information together by pulling context from various tools, or we rely on the memories of individuals involved. More often than not, when we depend on the latter, we experience the pitfalls of human biases. Scattered across disparate tools, communication channels, and individual notes, the "why" behind our actions becomes obscured. This reliance on manually aggregating and interpreting information, whether from scattered sources or individual recollections, is prone to subjective biases, incomplete data, and ultimately, ineffective outcomes.

The Solution

In this modern era, where our minds are often fragmented across a multitude of tools and solutions, a simple, adaptable, and effective "frame" of "mind" is more necessary than ever to do our best "work". This need has driven the creation of W.A.D.E, a unifying "framework" for doing "work," which incentivizes clear ownership decisions, transparency of rationale, and, above all, minimizes the pitfalls of human-biased subjective narratives. By establishing a single source of truth that promotes these principles, this framework enables us to make thoughtful and effective decisions, leading to more impactful choices with greater clarity, alignment, and confidence.

Key Ideas

orkstreams

Workstream is similar to Projects in the classic sense. Workstreams are related to other workstreams by hierarchical relations. They have three key types of constructs: decisions, events & actions.

ctions

Actions are performed by a user on a workstream which drive it towards its intended goal.

ecisions

Decisions enforce the user to take ownership and capture intent with clarity and purpose. Decisions are the only constructs which can change the state of a workstream, encouraging thoughtfulness.

vents

Events are recorded on a workstream for context and awareness.
